## 🌱 Introduction

Climate shocks—floods, droughts, and heatwaves—are accelerating faster than systems designed to protect children.

In vulnerable regions, the consequences are immediate and severe:

- Water contamination → cholera outbreaks  
- Drought → food insecurity → malnutrition  
- Heatwaves → dehydration and child mortality  

Yet frontline health workers often receive **late, generic, or unusable information**.

---

## 🚨 Why This Matters

> Climate risks are not invisible.  
> They are **disconnected from action**.

Existing systems:

- Focus on **weather**, not **children**
- Provide **data**, not **decisions**
- Alert too late—or without guidance

**OpenChildRisk OS bridges this gap.**
